Finding a good exam simulator is harder than it looks. There are dozens of platforms promising realistic mock tests, but most either have outdated question banks, poor interfaces, or lock everything useful behind a paywall. We went through the most popular options — and verified each one directly — to save you time.

Whether you need a free mock exam for a professional certification, a competitive entrance exam, or an academic final, there is something on this list for you. One important note throughout: several platforms advertise **free** access but require registration or login. We flag these clearly so there are no surprises.

## 1. ExamOven — Best Distraction-Free Exam Simulator

**Website:** [examoven.com](https://examoven.com)  
**Price:** 100% Free — no paid tiers  
**Login required:** No — no account needed to practise  
**Best for:** Students who want a realistic, privacy-first exam environment with no tracking

ExamOven is a fully free, exam simulator that lets you take timed mock tests without creating an account. The platform's standout design choice is its zero-surveillance approach — ExamOven explicitly does not lock your browser, does not monitor your camera or screen, and does not track background processes. It positions itself as a self-study tool, not a proctoring service, which means practice is entirely on the honour system.

The simulator covers a broad range of subjects drawn from an open GitHub repository — including IELTS, JEE, NIMCET, BACEM, TELCAT, and others. You can also upload your own question sets in JSON format or use the AI-assist feature to generate custom practice questions. After each mock exam, you get detailed results with correct/incorrect breakdowns and visual performance charts.

Your data stays in your browser by default using local storage. If you want to practise across devices, you can opt in to sync your progress to your own Google Drive — entirely optional, no account required for standard use.

**What we like**
- No login, no account, no tracking — start practising immediately
- Open-source question banks on GitHub for full transparency
- Supports custom JSON uploads and AI-generated question sets
- Detailed results with charts and answer review
- Covers a wide range of academic and entrance exams

**What could be better**
- Question bank coverage varies significantly by subject
- Interface requires JavaScript — will not load without it enabled

**Verdict:** The best option for no-strings-attached mock exam practice. No account, no payment, no surveillance. If you want to simulate exam conditions on your own terms, start here.

## 2. Examzify — Best for Understanding Your Mistakes

**Website:** [examzify.com](https://examzify.com)  
**Price:** Free (premium plans available)  
**Login required:** Yes — account required to access practice content  
**Best for:** Learners who want detailed explanations, not just a score

Examzify's core strength is what happens after you answer a question. Most mock test platforms tell you whether you were right or wrong. Examzify explains *why* — walking through the reasoning behind the correct answer and why each distractor is incorrect. For subjects requiring genuine understanding rather than rote memorisation, this approach accelerates learning in a way that simple right/wrong feedback does not.

The platform covers a wide range of professional certifications and academic subjects. Questions are clearly written and consistently at an appropriate difficulty level. An account is required to access the practice content, but registration is free and straightforward.

**What we like**
- Explanation-first approach to both correct and incorrect answers
- High question quality with useful hints
- Works on mobile and desktop
- Broad subject coverage across professional certifications

**What could be better**
- Requires account creation before accessing any practice content
- Advanced analytics behind a paid subscription

**Verdict:** A solid choice if you want to understand material, not just memorise it. Be prepared to register before you can access questions.

## 4. PracticeMock — Best for Performance Analytics

**Website:** [practicemock.com](https://practicemock.com)  
**Price:** 1 free full-length mock test per exam + paid test series  
**Login required:** Yes — mandatory even for the free test  
**Best for:** Data-driven learners, competitive exam candidates, anyone who wants detailed score analysis

PracticeMock takes the position that taking a mock test matters less than understanding what your score actually means. The platform provides one free full-length mock test per exam, after which full test series access requires a paid subscription.

Where PracticeMock genuinely excels is post-test analytics. After completing an exam simulator session, you get your All India Rank among 20 lakh+ users, a topic-by-topic accuracy breakdown, and time-per-question data that reveals pacing problems. Detailed question explanations, however, are reserved for paid subscribers.

**What we like**
- All India Rank among 20 lakh+ registered users
- Time-per-question data reveals pacing weaknesses
- Covers banking, SSC, Railways, Regulatory (RBI, SEBI, NABARD) exams
- Free test closely mirrors the actual exam interface

**What could be better**
- Signup required for all users — no guest access
- Only one free full-length test per exam; additional tests require payment
- Detailed explanations and full analytics behind a paid subscription

**Verdict:** Worth registering for if you want benchmark data on where you stand relative to other competitive exam candidates. Just be clear-eyed that the free offering is limited to one test per exam type.

## 6. Google Forms / Microsoft Forms — Best DIY Exam Simulator

**Website:** [forms.google.com](https://forms.google.com) / [forms.microsoft.com](https://forms.microsoft.com)  
**Price:** Free  
**Login required:** Yes — requires a Google or Microsoft account  
**Best for:** Teachers creating custom mock tests, self-directed learners building their own question sets

Google Forms and Microsoft Forms are not exam simulators in the traditional sense — they are general-purpose form builders that can be configured as custom mock tests. For teachers and tutors, this is genuinely powerful: you can build a test tailored exactly to what you have been teaching, share it as a link, and receive automatic scoring and response data without any specialist software.

Neither platform was designed as a professional exam simulator, so they lack timed lockdown modes, peer benchmarking, and format accuracy for specific professional tests. For custom practice and teacher-created assessments, however, they are the most flexible free tools available.

**What we like**
- Completely free with a Google or Microsoft account
- Fully customisable — your own questions, your own format
- Easy to share via link with automatic grading
- Works on any device

**What could be better**
- No pre-built question banks — you create all content yourself
- Lacks timed exam lockdown, detailed analytics, and format accuracy for professional certifications

**Verdict:** Not a replacement for a dedicated exam simulator, but the best free tool for creating your own custom mock tests.

## 7. Quizlet — Best for Flashcard-Based Subject Review

**Website:** [quizlet.com](https://quizlet.com)  
**Price:** Free (flashcards only); Quizlet Plus at $7.99/month or $35.99/year  
**Login required:** Yes — account required  
**Best for:** Vocabulary, definitions, factual recall, language learning — as a supplement to a proper exam simulator

Quizlet remains the largest flashcard platform in the world, with millions of user-created study sets covering almost every subject imaginable. However, the free tier is now substantially more limited than it was in previous years. The Learn mode — where Quizlet's evidence-based active recall happens — is capped at five free rounds per study set, and practice test generation is limited to one free test per set.

**What we like**
- Massive library of user-created study sets
- Flashcards remain unlimited and free
- Mobile apps are well-designed and reliable

**What could be better**
- Free tier now significantly limited — Learn mode and practice tests are capped
- Many previously free features now require Quizlet Plus
- User-generated content quality varies — always verify accuracy before trusting
- Not suitable as a primary exam simulator for professional or complex academic exams

**Verdict:** Still useful for vocabulary and recall-heavy subjects as a supplement. Be aware the free tier is now limited, and if you need mock exam functionality, budget for a subscription.

## 9. Anki — Best Spaced Repetition for Long-Term Retention

**Website:** [apps.ankiweb.net](https://apps.ankiweb.net)  
**Price:** Free on desktop (Windows, Mac, Linux) and Android; one-time paid fee on iOS  
**Login required:** No — optional AnkiWeb account for cloud sync  
**Best for:** Long-term retention, medical/law school, languages, any exam requiring deep memorisation

Anki is technically a flashcard app, not an exam simulator — but it earns its place on this list because its spaced repetition system is the most scientifically validated method available for building long-term retention, which is the foundation that all other exam preparation depends on.

Anki shows you a card, you rate how well you remembered it, and the algorithm schedules the next review based on your rating. Cards you find easy are shown less frequently; cards you struggle with come back sooner. Over time, this produces retention rates significantly higher than traditional review methods.

**What we like**
- Spaced repetition is scientifically the most effective memorisation method
- Massive shared deck library covering medicine, law, languages, and more
- Free on desktop and Android

**What could be better**
- iOS app (AnkiMobile) costs a one-time fee
- Dated interface — steeper initial learning curve
- Not an exam simulator — must be paired with a proper mock test platform

**Verdict:** Not a standalone exam simulator, but an essential companion for any exam requiring significant memorisation. Medical and law students should use this alongside their primary mock test platform.

## 10. LawHub — Best Free Official LSAT Practice Platform

**Website:** [lawhub.org](https://lawhub.org)  
**Price:** Free tier (4 practice tests); LawHub Advantage ~$120/year (58+ tests)  
**Login required:** Yes — LSAC account required  
**Best for:** LSAT preparation with official practice materials from LSAC

> [!NOTE]
> As of June 2024, the official LSAT practice tests and interactive exercises that were previously on Khan Academy moved to LawHub, operated by the Law School Admission Council (LSAC) — the body that actually administers the LSAT.

LawHub provides full-length practice LSATs using the same interface as the actual exam, which matters because the LSAT is a computer-based test with a specific navigation style. Practising in the exact test environment removes one variable of uncertainty on exam day. A free LSAC account is required, but this is the same account you will need to register for the exam itself.

The free tier is genuinely useful but limited — you get 4 full official PrepTests. For serious preparation, LawHub Advantage ($120/year) unlocks a library of 58+ previously administered official tests. If you have an LSAC fee waiver, LawHub Advantage is available at no cost.

**What we like**
- Official LSAC platform — practice tests are authentic, not third-party approximations
- Same interface as the real exam
- Free account includes 4 full official PrepTests to start
- Updated to reflect the 2024 revised LSAT format

**What could be better**
- Free tier is limited to 4 practice tests — serious prep likely requires LawHub Advantage
- LawHub Advantage costs ~$120/year (free with LSAC fee waiver)
- Account creation required
- Focused exclusively on LSAT — not useful for other exam types

**Verdict:** If you are preparing for the LSAT, LawHub is now the correct place to go. The free tier gives you 4 official tests to start; budget for LawHub Advantage if you need the full library. Khan Academy no longer hosts the interactive LSAT practice content.

## Final Thoughts: Choosing the Right Exam Simulator or Mock Exam Platform

The best exam simulator for you depends on what exam you are actually taking and how much friction you are willing to accept. Here is a quick decision guide:

- **Competitive banking/government exam in India** → ixamBee or PracticeMock (both require registration)
- **CompTIA certification** → ExamCompass (no login, free) paired with a full-length timed mock exam
- **SAT** → Khan Academy (official partnership, free)
- **LSAT** → LawHub (official LSAC platform, free with account)
- **Custom mock test or teacher-created assessments** → Google Forms or Microsoft Forms
- **Distraction-free self-study with no account** → **ExamOven** (**Editor's Choice**)
- **Long-term memorisation for medical or law school** → Anki alongside any other platform
- **Vocabulary or recall-heavy subjects** → Quizlet (free tier is now limited — be aware)

> [!TIP]
> Several platforms on this list advertise free access prominently but require registration before you can take a single test. If you want genuinely zero-barrier practice — no email, no account, no password — **ExamOven** and **ExamCompass** are the only options on this list that deliver that.

Every platform here has at least some free access worth exploring before committing to a paid tier. Pick the one that matches your exam type, run a full timed mock test in your first week of preparation, and build your study routine from there.
