## Why Mock Tests Are Non-Negotiable
In the world of competitive exams, knowledge is only half the battle. The other half is **strategy**, **speed**, and **stamina**. This is where online mock tests come in. They are not just practice; they are a rehearsal for the main event.

Whether you are aiming for an MCA seat through **NIMCET** or **CUET**, or chasing a government job via **SSC** or **Banking** exams, the principles of effective practice remain the same.

## 1. Simulate the Real Environment
The biggest mistake students make is taking mock tests casually. Lying on your bed with a phone in one hand and a snack in the other is *not* practice.

*   **Sit at a Desk:** Mimic the exam hall posture.
*   **Use a Laptop/Desktop:** Most exams are Computer Based Tests (CBT). Practicing on a mobile screen is different from navigating a mouse and keyboard.
*   **Strict Timing:** Never pause the timer. If you need a break, the clock keeps tickingâ€”just like in the real exam.

## 2. Analyze, Don't Just Attempt
Taking a test takes 2 hours. Analyzing it should take at least 3. If you aren't analyzing, you aren't improving.

### The 3-Step Analysis Framework:
1.  **The Silly Mistakes:** Questions you knew but got wrong due to calculation errors or misreading. *Fix:* Slow down and read carefully.
2.  **The Knowledge Gaps:** Questions you didn't know at all. *Fix:* Go back to your textbooks and study that specific topic.
3.  **The Time Traps:** Questions you got right but took too long (e.g., 5 minutes for a 1-minute question). *Fix:* Find a shortcut or learn to skip.

## 3. Accuracy > Speed (Initially)
In the beginning, focus on getting questions right. Speed comes naturally with confidence and familiarity. A student who attempts 50 questions with 90% accuracy will always beat a student who attempts 80 questions with 60% accuracy (thanks to negative marking!).

## 4. The Art of Skipping
You don't get extra marks for solving the hardest question in the paper. In fact, getting stuck on a tough problem is a trap set by the examiner.

*   **Rule of Thumb:** If you can't see a path to the solution within the first 30 seconds, **Mark for Review** and move on.
*   Come back to these "Challenge Questions" only after you have secured the easy marks.

## 5. Consistency is Key
Don't binge-practice 5 tests in one day and then do nothing for a week.
*   **Frequency:** Take 1-2 full-length mock tests per week.
*   **Routine:** Try to take the mock test at the same time of day as your actual exam slot (e.g., 9 AM - 12 PM) to train your biological clock.
