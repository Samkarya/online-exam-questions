## What Is CAT? (And Why Does It Matter So Much?)

CAT is a national-level Computer Based Test conducted annually for admission to postgraduate management programs (MBA/PGP). It is the gateway to the **21 IIMs** — which collectively offer approximately **5,500+ PGP/MBA seats** — and over **1,200 non-IIM participating institutions**, including well-regarded B-schools like FMS Delhi, SPJIMR Mumbai, and MDI Gurgaon.

Here's the number that puts everything in perspective: in CAT 2025, approximately **2.95 lakh candidates registered**, out of which **~2.58 lakh actually appeared** on exam day. The competition is intense, the margin for error is thin, and your CAT score is valid for **one year only** — making preparation urgency very real.

To put the cutoffs in context: getting into IIM Ahmedabad, Bangalore, or Calcutta requires a **99+ percentile**. IIM Lucknow, Indore, and Kozhikode typically demand **97–98 percentile**. Even newer IIMs hover around **90–94 percentile** as their floor.

> **Sources:** [CollegeDekho — IIM Selection Criteria](https://www.collegedekho.com/articles/iim-selection-criteria-cat-score-requirements-for-iim-seats/) | [CollegeDunia — CAT Overview](https://collegedunia.com/exams/cat)

## CAT 2026 Eligibility Criteria

### Educational Qualification

- **Minimum Degree:** A Bachelor's degree (or equivalent) from a recognized university or institution.
- **Minimum Marks:** 50% aggregate or equivalent CGPA for General, NC-OBC, and EWS candidates. 45% aggregate for SC, ST, and PwD candidates.
- **Final-Year Students:** You can apply even if results are pending. However, admission remains provisional until your final marksheet and degree certificate are submitted and verified.
- **Professional Degrees:** Holders of CA, CS, or ICWA qualifications are eligible, provided they meet the minimum percentage criteria.
- **Age Limit:** There is no minimum or maximum age limit for CAT.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Meeting the minimum percentage does **not** guarantee a shortlist call. Each IIM independently verifies academic eligibility at the interview stage — candidates who misrepresent their scores face disqualification.

> **Source:** [TopRankers — CAT 2026](https://www.toprankers.com/cat-exam) | [LPTedtech — CAT 2026](https://lptedtech.com/blog/cat/2026-details/)

### Reservation Policy

SC, ST, and PwD candidates are eligible with **45% aggregate marks** or equivalent CGPA. Category certificates must be valid and in the Government of India format for the current financial year.

## CAT 2026 Registration: Step-by-Step Guide

Registration opens **August 1, 2026**, entirely online at [iimcat.ac.in](https://iimcat.ac.in). No offline applications are accepted.

### Step 1 — Create Your Profile
Visit the official CAT portal. Click "New Candidate Registration." Enter your name, date of birth, email ID, and mobile number to receive your **User ID and Password**.

### Step 2 — Fill the Application Form
Log in and enter your personal and contact details, category information (General / NC-OBC / EWS / SC / ST / PwD), academic qualifications (Class 10, 12, and graduation), and any full-time post-graduation work experience.

### Step 3 — Select Programs and Test Cities
Choose your preferred IIM campuses and MBA/PGP programs. Select **up to 6 test city preferences** in order of priority. You'll be allotted one city based on availability.

### Step 4 — Upload Documents

Upload scanned copies of the following before submitting:

- Passport-sized photograph (white background)
- Signature
- Category / PwD certificate (if applicable)

Specific file size and dimension requirements will be in the 2026 notification bulletin on iimcat.ac.in.

### Step 5 — Pay the Fee and Submit
Pay online via credit card, debit card, net banking, or UPI. Download the **confirmation page** after successful payment.

> [!CAUTION]
> Changes to your name, date of birth, or category are **not allowed** after final submission. The fee is **non-refundable** under any circumstance.

A **correction/edit window** opens in the last week of September 2026, allowing limited changes to your photograph, signature, and test city preferences.

## CAT 2026 Syllabus: Section-by-Section

The CAT does not have a rigidly "prescribed" syllabus the way board exams do. What it does have is a very consistent pattern of topic areas. Here's what actually shows up:

### 1. Verbal Ability & Reading Comprehension (VARC)

VARC is often misunderstood as a "language" section. It is actually a **reasoning and comprehension** section that heavily rewards analytical reading over raw vocabulary.

- **Reading Comprehension (RC):** 3–4 long passages per exam, testing inference, tone, author intent, and main argument — not surface-level recall.
- **Para Jumbles:** Re-ordering sentences into a coherent paragraph. Often in TITA format (no negative marking).
- **Para Summary:** Choose the best summary of a given paragraph from 4 options.
- **Odd Sentence Out:** Identify the sentence that doesn't belong in a paragraph.
- **Sentence Completion:** Less frequent, but occasionally appears.

> [!TIP]
> Most high-scorers in VARC are not "English people" — they are structured thinkers who practice reading dense analytical writing regularly. Build the habit early.

### 2. Data Interpretation & Logical Reasoning (DILR)

This section has evolved to become the most variable in difficulty. It is no longer about speed alone — it's about **set selection strategy**.

- **Data Interpretation:** Tables, bar graphs, pie charts, line graphs, caselets, Venn Diagrams (3 and 4 circles), and network diagrams.
- **Logical Reasoning:** Seating arrangements (linear and circular), blood relations, scheduling problems, binary logic, routes and maps, constraint-based puzzles.

> [!TIP]
> In any given DILR section, 2 out of 4 sets are often significantly harder than the other 2. The test-taker who correctly identifies and skips the hard sets wins the section.

### 3. Quantitative Ability (QA)

Arithmetic and Algebra together have historically comprised **over 60% of QA questions**. Geometry and Number Systems make up much of the rest.

- **Arithmetic:** Percentages, Profit & Loss, Simple & Compound Interest, Time-Speed-Distance, Time & Work, Mixtures & Alligation.
- **Algebra:** Linear and Quadratic Equations, Logarithms, Functions, Inequalities, Progressions.
- **Geometry & Mensuration:** Triangles, Circles, Polygons, Coordinate Geometry, Area and Volume.
- **Number Systems:** Remainders, HCF/LCM, Properties of Integers, Factorials.
- **Modern Math:** Permutation & Combination, Probability, Sequences & Series, Set Theory.

> **Source:** [SuperGrads / TopRankers — CAT Syllabus](https://www.toprankers.com/cat-exam)

## CAT 2026 Preparation Strategy

There is no shortage of CAT advice on the internet. Most of it says the same things in different orders. What follows is a phased, honest strategy — one that acknowledges that your time and energy are finite.

---

### Phase 1: Foundation Building (January – May)

**Goal:** Build conceptual clarity from scratch. No shortcuts yet.

- **QA:** Start with Arithmetic. Master percentages, ratios, and TSD before moving to anything else — these alone make up a large chunk of the paper. Use *Arun Sharma (McGraw Hill)* or *Sarvesh Verma's Quantum CAT*.
- **VARC:** Read one article a day from long-form publications: *Aeon*, *The Economist*, *The Atlantic*, or *The Guardian*. Don't time yourself. Focus on following arguments, not just words.
- **DILR:** Solve 2 sets a day without a timer. The goal is to understand puzzle structures, not score points yet. You can find high-quality practice sets in the **[ExamOven DILR Library](/exam-library?q=cat)**.

> [!TIP]
> End-of-phase check: By May, you should be able to solve any Arithmetic problem cold — without having to "think about what formula applies."

---

### Phase 2: Sectional Testing & Weak Area Work (June – August)

**Goal:** Convert understanding into exam performance.

- Begin **Sectional Tests** (VARC-only, DILR-only, QA-only) to identify specific weak spots.
- Start a **weekly mock test** schedule from August. Take mocks in your actual expected exam time slot — your brain needs to be conditioned to think hard at that specific hour.
- Practice **set selection** in DILR. Scan all 4 sets in the first 4–5 minutes before diving into any.
- In QA, start a **formula revision book** — a small notebook with all key formulas. Review it for 20 minutes every morning.

> [!TIP]
> End-of-phase check: Your mock scores should be consistently improving. More importantly, you should be able to explain *why* you got each wrong answer wrong.

---

### Phase 3: Mock Test Intensification & Refinement (September – November)

**Goal:** Peak performance on exam day.

- Increase to **two mocks per week**. After each mock, spend at least 2× the time you spent writing the mock on analysis. Review every question you attempted — correct and incorrect. The **[ExamOven CAT PYQs](/exam-library?q=cat)** provide AI-driven insights into your performance.
- Re-solve difficult DILR sets without looking at solutions first. This is more valuable than attempting new sets.
- For VARC, stop trying to "improve reading speed." Instead, focus on understanding passage *structure* — where is the main argument? Where is the author using examples vs. evidence?
- Two weeks before the exam: reduce new learning, increase revision. Sleep well. Maintain your testing routine.

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

**In DILR:**
- Spending 25+ minutes on a single difficult set and running out of time. Always scan all 4 sets first.
- Attempting sets in the order they appear on screen (the hardest set often appears first).

**In VARC:**
- Reading RC passages word-by-word instead of focusing on argument flow and paragraph function.
- Ignoring TITA questions in Para Jumbles — zero negative marking means you should always attempt them.

**In QA:**
- Attempting every question in sequence. Skip time-consuming trap questions immediately.
- Neglecting Geometry — it's harder to learn late, but it reliably appears every year.

**In General:**
- Obsessing over mock test percentile scores instead of deeply analyzing why you got answers wrong.
- Comparing preparation levels with peers. Your exam is individual; your analysis should be too.

## After CAT: What Happens Next?

Cracking CAT is just step one. Here's the broader IIM admission pipeline:

1. **CAT Result (January 2027):** Scores are normalized across the three exam slots, then converted to percentiles.
2. **IIM Shortlisting:** Each IIM independently shortlists candidates based on its own composite score — CAT percentile, academic record (Class 10, 12, graduation), work experience, and diversity factors. Criteria differ significantly between IIMs.
3. **WAT/GD + PI:** Shortlisted candidates appear for Written Ability Test (WAT) or Group Discussion (GD) and Personal Interview (PI).
4. **Final Merit List:** IIMs release final admission offers based on a weighted composite of all stages.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> As an example: IIM Indore's 2026–28 admission criteria allocate **20% weight to the CAT score** in the final merit list, with **74% weight on Class 10 and 12 academic performance**, and 6% for diversity. Strong CAT performance alone is necessary but not sufficient.
>
> **Source:** [CollegeDekho — IIM Indore Selection Criteria](https://www.collegedekho.com/articles/iim-indore-selection-criteria/)
